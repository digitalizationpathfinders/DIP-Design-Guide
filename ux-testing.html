UX Testing

UX testing guidance

Step-by-step guidance for recruiting participants, writing realistic tasks, and running test cycles that improve webform usability.

Recruit participants

Recruit people who represent your audience. Use short screening questions and seek a mix of experience levels.

  • Work with stakeholders to identify target user groups.
  • Use email, internal networks, or existing customer panels to invite participants.
  • Keep recruitment messaging clear and respectful of participant time.

Consent and ethics

Provide a short consent form describing the purpose, what participants will do, and how their data will be used.

  • Explain voluntary participation and the ability to stop at any time.
  • Record consent before testing begins.
  • Keep personal data only as long as needed and store it securely.

Write good task scenarios

Keep tasks realistic, simple, and focused on goals rather than exact steps.

  • Use everyday language: "You need to apply for..." rather than "Click through to..."
  • Avoid overly prescriptive instructions that guide the participant too much.
  • Keep the task goal clear and provide only enough context for realism.

Typical test process

  1. Initial mockups and concept review
  2. Heuristic analysis and expert review
  3. Baseline usability testing with early prototypes
  4. Report findings and prioritize improvements
  5. Iterate designs and run a second round of testing
